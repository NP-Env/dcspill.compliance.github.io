CERCLA §302.4 Statutory Codes (source of CERCLA designation): 1 = CWA §311(b)(2) Hazardous Substance · 2 = CWA §307(a) Toxic Pollutant · 3 = CAA §112 Hazardous Air Pollutant · 4 = RCRA §3001 Hazardous Waste.  ·  EPCRA App. A TPQ = Extremely Hazardous Substance Threshold Planning Quantity (40 CFR Part 355, App. A).  ·  Part 112 thresholds (1,000 gal / 42 gal) refer to oil reaching navigable waters or adjoining shorelines. MS4s are evaluated as conveyance pathways under SPCC applicability.
🟠 Arizona: ADEQ’s “any quantity impacting a waterway” rule applies to all liquid substances regardless of federal RQ. HFC refrigerants are exempt as gases. ARS §49-128 requires immediate oral ADEQ notification in addition to NRC — written follow-up (30 days) flows from EPCRA §304(c).

CERCLA §103Hazardous Substance Release — 40 CFR §302.6
TriggerRelease at or above RQ within any 24-hour period
Who must reportPerson in charge of the vessel or facility
Notify
National Response Center (NRC) — immediately
Phone: 1-800-424-8802 (24/7) · DC area: (202) 267-2675 · nrc.uscg.mil
Written follow-upWritten report to the EPA Regional Administrator as soon as practicable. Content: facility name/location, max storage capacity, corrective actions taken, SPCC plan compliance status. (40 CFR §302.6)
TimingInitial notification: immediate. Written report: as soon as practicable.
Applicable substancesEthylene glycol (5,000 lbs) · Sulfuric acid (1,000 lbs) · Potassium hydroxide (1,000 lbs) · Lead (10 lbs)
ExemptionsFederally permitted releases; solid metal particles >100 µm; <1,000 lb/24 hr NOₓ from combustion
CERCLA §103(f)(2)Continuous Release — Reduced Reporting / 40 CFR §302.8
Applies whenRelease is continuous (routine, without interruption) and stable in quantity and rate — qualifies for reduced reporting in lieu of per-release NRC notification
Notify
NRC — initial telephone: 1-800-424-8802
SERC + LEPC of any state likely to be affected
Written follow-up Initial written report to EPA HQ Office within 30 days of initial NRC call.

First anniversary follow-up within 30 days of anniversary date — to EPA HQ Office.

Annual written reports thereafter to EPA HQ Office + SERC + LEPC. Non-CERCLA EHS continuous releases: written to SERC and LEPC only. (40 CFR §302.8)
TimingInitial telephone: immediate. Initial written: within 30 days. Annual: within 30 days of each anniversary date.
NoteNon-CERCLA EHS continuous releases: notify SERC and LEPC only — NOT the NRC
EPCRA §304Emergency Release Notification — 40 CFR Part 355
TriggerRelease of a CERCLA hazardous substance or EPCRA EHS at or above its RQ beyond the facility boundary
Notify
1. NRC — immediately: 1-800-424-8802
2. SERC — immediately
epa.gov/epcra/state-emergency-response-commissions-contacts · EPCRA Info Center: 1-800-424-9346
3. LEPC — immediately
epa.gov/epcra/finding-your-lepc
Written follow-upWritten report to SERC and LEPC as soon as practicable. No federal format specified — email acceptable. Must include: updates to initial notice, response actions taken, medical attention advice. State deadlines vary. (EPCRA §304(c))
Written follow-upWritten report to AZSERC and local LEPC within 30 days. Email acceptable.

Submit to AZSERC: azserc@azdeq.gov · Phone: 602-771-4065
Submit to local LEPC: see AZSERC LEPC Members (PDF)

Source: azdeq.gov/emergency-release-reporting
TimingInitial notification: immediate. Written follow-up: within 30 days (AZ); as soon as practicable (federal floor).
Applicable substancesAll CERCLA hazardous substances (ethylene glycol, sulfuric acid, potassium hydroxide, lead) and EPCRA EHS (sulfuric acid)
Oil — 40 CFR Part 110Discharge of Oil — “Sheen Rule” — CWA §311(b)(3)/(b)(4)
Trigger (§110.3)Any oil discharge causing: (a) violation of water quality standards, OR (b) film/sheen/discoloration on navigable water or shoreline, OR (c) sludge/emulsion beneath surface or on shorelines. No minimum volume.
Notify
National Response Center (NRC) — immediately
Phone: 1-800-424-8802 (24/7) · nrc.uscg.mil
Written follow-upNone required under 40 CFR Part 110. Telephone notification to NRC is the sole obligation. If the discharge also meets SPCC §112.4 thresholds, that section’s written requirements apply.
TimingImmediately upon knowledge of discharge
Applicable substancesDiesel fuel · Engine lubricating oil · Hydraulic fluid · Transformer oil · Mineral oil (immersion)
Oil — 40 CFR Part 112 (SPCC)Oil Pollution Prevention — SPCC Rule — §112.4 Reporting
Trigger (§112.4)>1,000 U.S. gallons in a single discharge, OR >42 U.S. gallons in each of two discharges within any 12-month period — oil reaching navigable waters or adjoining shorelines
Notify
1. NRC — immediately: 1-800-424-8802
2. State oil pollution control agency — simultaneously with EPA RA written report
Written follow-upWritten report to the EPA Regional Administrator within 60 days of the discharge. Content: facility name/location, max storage capacity, corrective actions taken, SPCC plan compliance status. Submit simultaneously to the state oil pollution control agency. (40 CFR §112.4(a))
TimingNRC notification: immediate. Written report to EPA RA: within 60 days.
Applicable substancesDiesel fuel · Engine lubricating oil · Hydraulic fluid · Transformer oil · Mineral oil (immersion)
🟠 Arizona — ARS §49-128State Parallel Oral Notification — ADEQ & LEPC (All CERCLA / EHS Releases)
AuthorityArizona Revised Statutes §49-128 — Emergency notification of reportable releases
TriggerRelease of a CERCLA-listed hazardous substance or EPCRA EHS at or above its reportable quantity
Notification typeOral only. ARS §49-128 mandates immediate oral notification. No written follow-up requirement in the statute itself.
Notify
ADEQ Emergency Response — immediately (oral, in addition to NRC)
Phone: (602) 771-2330 (24/7) · Toll-free: 1-800-234-5677
azdeq.gov/emergency-release-reporting
LEPC community emergency coordinator — immediately (oral)
azdeq.gov/azserc · AZSERC LEPC Members (PDF)
Written follow-upObligation flows from federal EPCRA §304(c) — written report to AZSERC and local LEPC within 30 days.

Submit to AZSERC: azserc@azdeq.gov · Phone: 602-771-4065
TimingOral: immediately. Written follow-up: within 30 days.
Key distinctionCalling the NRC does not satisfy ARS §49-128. Both are independent, parallel obligations. Applicable: Ethylene glycol · Sulfuric acid · Potassium hydroxide · Lead.
🟠 Arizona — ADEQ / ARS §49-1004Any Quantity to Waterway & Petroleum to Soil / Groundwater (All USTs)
AuthorityADEQ general spill reporting policy (azdeq.gov/ReportSpill); A.R.S. §49-1004 (UST petroleum releases)
Waterway triggerAny liquid substance, any quantity reaching an Arizona waterway must be reported to ADEQ.
UST petroleum triggerAny confirmed or suspected petroleum release to soil or groundwater from a UST — report within 24 hours. Exception: ≤25 gal contained and cleaned up within 24 hours.
Notify
ADEQ Emergency Response — immediately
Phone: (602) 771-2330 (24/7) · azdeq.gov/ReportSpill
NRC — if federal Part 110 sheen rule is also triggered: 1-800-424-8802
Written follow-upUST releases — initial written Release Status Report within 14 calendar days. Content per ARS §49-1004(C) and A.A.C. R18-12-260(C).

90-day follow-up report within 90 calendar days of ADEQ’s notification letter.
GasesHFC refrigerants (R-410A, R-134a, R-32) — waterway trigger does not apply.
ADEQ guidance“If in doubt, report the spill.” — ADEQ, azdeq.gov/ReportSpill
🟠 Arizona groundwater context: Desert soils provide minimal natural attenuation — ADEQ applies a precautionary approach to protect Arizona’s aquifers.
